prefLabel: CGI Fault Movement Sense Term Vocabulary
definition: This file contains the CGI Fault Movement Sense vocabulary. Terms to specify the relative direction of movement of bodies of rock across a fault (GeoSciML ShearDisplacementStructure) surface. Examples include 'sinistral', 'dextral', 'normal', 'reverse'.

    URI http://resource.geosciml.org/classifier/cgi/faultmovementsense/detachment

    URI http://resource.geosciml.org/classifier/cgi/faultmovementsense/dextral
    Preferred label (en) dextral
    Definition () Right-lateral separation sense; in plan view, the side opposite the observer appears displaced to the right.
    Source () GGIC vocabularies working group (Australia)
    Relationships:
    Exact match http://resource.geosciml.org/classifier/cgi/faultmovementsense/0002

    URI http://resource.geosciml.org/classifier/cgi/faultmovementsense/generic_decollement
    Preferred label (en) generic decollement
    Definition () A large-displacement (kilometers or tens of kilometers) shallowly dipping to subhorizontal fault or shear zone.
    Source () Neuendorf et al. 2005
    Relationships:
    Exact match http://resource.geosciml.org/classifier/cgi/faultmovementsense/0003

    ⇑ -- no movement sense --

    URI http://resource.geosciml.org/classifier/cgi/faultmovementsense/no_movement_sense
    Preferred label (en) no movement sense
    Definition () The fault-parallel displacement is effectively zero, as in an extraction fault.
    Source () Froitzheim, N., Pleuger, J. & Nagel, T.J., 2006. Extraction faults. Journal of Structural Geology v28, pp. 1388-1395.
    Relationships:
    Exact match http://resource.geosciml.org/classifier/cgi/faultmovementsense/0004
