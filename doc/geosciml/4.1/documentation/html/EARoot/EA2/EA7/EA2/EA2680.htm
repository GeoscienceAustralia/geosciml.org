Sampling manifold schema<br/><br/>When observations are made to estimate properties of a spatially extensive feature, in particular where the value of a property varies within the scope of the feature, a spatial sampling feature is used. Depending on accessibility and on the nature of the expected property variation, the sampling feature may be extensive in one, two or three dimensions. Processing and visualization methods are often dependent on the dimensionality of the sampling manifold, so this provides a natural classification system for sampling features.  <br/>This classification follows common practice in focussing on conventional spatial dimensions. Properties observed on sampling features may be time-dependent, but the temporal axis does not generally contribute to the classification of sampling feature classes. Sampling feature identity is usually less time-dependent than property value. <br/><br/>A Spatial Sampling Feature may host observations (a) that concern the whole feature, in which case the result may be constant, or may be a Coverage whose value varies across its extent (b) at locations contained within the SamplingFeature. <br/><br/>The shape of a Spatial Sampling Feature provides a manifold within which sub-sampling may be undertaken. The shape may be used as the datum for an engineering coordinate reference system, so that sub-samples may be conveniently located in the context of the parent features. <br/><br/>Furthermore, observations may concern the variation of a result on any spatio-temporal axis not fixed by the shape of the feature - e.g. if location is defined in 3 spatial dimensions, time-variation of observed properties is unbounded. <br/>
