<font color="#4f81bd">ParticleGeometryDescription</font> describes particles in a <font color="#4f81bd">CompoundMaterial</font> independent of their relationship to each other or their orientation. It is distinguished from <font color="#4f81bd">Fabric</font> in that the <font color="#4f81bd">ParticleGeometryDescription</font> remains constant if the material is disaggregated into its constituent particles, whereas <font color="#4f81bd">Fabric</font> is lost if the material is disaggregated. Properties include the particle size (grainsize), particle sorting (size distribution, e.g., well sorted, poorly sorted, bimodal sorting), particle shape (surface rounding or crystal face development, e.g., well rounded, euhedral, anhedral), and particle aspect ratio (e.g., elongated, platy, bladed, compact, acicular).<br/><br/>

Notes: The <font color="#4f81bd">particleType:ParticleTypeTerm</font> provides a term from a controlled vocabulary to specify the nature of individual particles of each constituent in an <font color="#4f81bd">EarthMaterial</font> aggregation, based mostly on their genesis.  When applied on <font color="#4f81bd">ParticleDescription</font> for <font color="#4f81bd">CompoundMaterial</font>, it would characterise all particles in aggregate. Use this property on <font color="#4f81bd">CompoundMaterial</font> to distinguish rocks composed of crystals (crystalline rocks) from rocks composed of granular particles (clasts, fragments). Examples include ooliths, crystals, pore space. Constituent type is determined based on the nature of the particles, and ideally is independent of the relationship between particles in a compound material aggregation.<br/><br/>

Notes: The <font color="#4f81bd">aspectRatio</font> property (<font color="#4f81bd">SWE::Category</font>) contains a term from a controlled vocabulary describing the geometry of particles based on the ratios of lengths of long, intermediate and short axes of grains. It equates to sphericity in sedimentary rocks (i.e., the degree to which the shape of a particle approximates a sphere).  The formal definition is “<i>A quantitative specification based on the ratio of lengths of long, intermediate and short axes of grain shape</i>”. (e.g., prolate, slightly flattened, very bladed, equant, acicular, tabular).<br/><br/>
