The <font color="#4f81bd">ConstituentPart</font> class describes how Earth materials may be made up of other Earth materials, including the proportion of the constituent part in the whole (e.g., 20%, minor, dominant); and the role that the constituent plays in the whole (e.g., matrix, groundmass, framework, phenocryst, xenolith, vein). <br/><br/>

Notes: The <font color="#4f81bd">role:ConstituentPartRoleTerm</font> property contains a term from a controlled vocabulary that describes the role a <font color="#4f81bd">ConstituentPart</font> plays in a <font color="#4f81bd">CompoundMaterial</font> aggregation. The same <font color="#4f81bd">EarthMaterial</font> may occur as different <font color="#4f81bd">ConstituentParts</font> playing different roles within one <font color="#4f81bd">CompoundMaterial</font>.  For example, feldspar may be present as groundmass (“groundmass” is a <font color="#4f81bd">ConstituentPart::role</font>) and as phenocrysts (“phenocryst” is another <font color="#4f81bd">ConstituentPart::role</font>) within a single igneous rock.<br/><br/>
